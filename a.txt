<div id="wholeLayer"><table align="left" border="0" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0" width="100%"><tbody><tr><td id="etext0" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;font-family:Arial;background-color:#f4f4f4">As far as Paris is concerned, Russia’s support of armed proxies in Ukraine and the ensuing destruction of Malaysia Airlines flight MH17 is no obstacle to delivery of the first of two French-built Mistral-class ships . And, for the moment, options on the delivery of the Vladivostok’s sister ship next year remain open, subject to Russian behaviour. Which raises the question of how bad Moscow’s behaviour has to become force a cancellation.</td><td rowspan="26" width="1%"></td><td id="ctext0" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;background-color:#f4f4f4"><strong></strong><!---->就法国而言，俄罗斯对其在乌克兰的武装代理人的支持，以及接下来的马来西亚航空公司(Malaysia Airlines) MH17航班被击落事件，都不构成法国向俄罗斯交付两艘“西北风”级(Mistral-class)攻击舰中的第一艘——“符拉迪沃斯托克”号(Vladivostok)——的障碍。而对于明年是否向俄罗斯交付该舰的姊妹舰，法国眼下仍有选择，这取决于俄罗斯的行为。这就引发了下列问题：俄罗斯的行为糟糕到何种程度会迫使法国取消交付。</td></tr><tr><td>&nbsp;</td><td>&nbsp;</td></tr><tr><td id="etext1" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;font-family:Arial;background-color:#f4f4f4">With Russia embarking on its neo-imperial enterprise, common sense militates against delivering tools of war that could put Nato allies at risk. So why is the deal proceeding, and how could the French be brought to cancel it?</td><td id="ctext1" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;background-color:#f4f4f4">俄罗斯正开启它的新帝国主义事业，依据常理，不应向俄罗斯交付可能会让北约(Nato)盟友陷入风险之中的战争工具。那么，这笔交易为何会推进，怎么能让法国取消交易呢？</td></tr><tr><td>&nbsp;</td><td>&nbsp;</td></tr><tr><td id="etext2" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;font-family:Arial;background-color:#f4f4f4">Shortly after Russia’s invasion of Georgia and President Barack Obama’s election in 2008, the US proposed a reset of relations with Moscow. Few western leaders took up this suggestion with as much gusto as Nicolas Sarkozy, then French president, with the sale of two naval assault ships of the new Mistral class for ￠1.2bn. The Mistral, although barely armed, has great military value as a highly versatile expeditionary command post, helicopter carrier and landing craft platform. It proved its worth when the French used it in the Libyan campaign of 2011, and as the pivot of the maritime component of Nato’s rapid response force.</td><td id="ctext2" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;background-color:#f4f4f4">在俄罗斯入侵格鲁吉亚以及巴拉克?奥巴马(Barack Obama)在2008年当选美国总统后不久，美国曾提出重建与俄罗斯的关系。对于这一提议，没几个西方领导人像法国当时的总统尼古拉?萨科齐(Nicolas Sarkozy)那样热情——他以12亿欧元向俄罗斯出售了两艘新型“西北风”级海军攻击舰。尽管并不配备武器，但作为用途极广的远征指挥站、直升机航母和登陆艇平台，“西北风”级攻击舰有着重要的军事价值。当法国在2011年利比亚战争中动用该型战舰时，作为北约快速反应部队海上力量的枢纽，它向世人证明了其价值。</td></tr><tr><td>&nbsp;</td><td>&nbsp;</td></tr><tr><td id="etext3" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;font-family:Arial;background-color:#f4f4f4">One reason the French may be reluctant to cancel is that the legal and economic penalties would be severe. The ships are built with Russian and French components – which means this is not a simple matter in which you pay back and indemnify the embargoed customer, then resell the kit to a third party. This helps explain why Paris is so vocal about the need for partners, notably the UK, to take similar pain. Laurent Fabius, the French foreign minister, has accused the UK of hypocrisy, given the weakness of financial sanctions against the Russian oligarchs who have made “Londongrad” their home.</td><td id="ctext3" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;background-color:#f4f4f4">法国可能不愿取消交易的一个原因是，取消交易将面临严重法律和经济惩罚。相关舰船在建造时使用了俄罗斯和法国零件，这意味着，这并不是简单的偿还价款并补偿被禁运的客户、然后将舰船重新卖给第三方的事情。这有利于解释法国为何强烈提出合作伙伴（尤其是英国）有必要承担同等的痛苦。法国外长洛朗?法比尤斯(Laurent Fabius)指责英国伪善，因为英国对俄罗斯寡头的金融制裁力度小，这些俄罗斯寡头已经把“伦敦格勒”(Londongrad)变成了他们的家。</td></tr><tr><td>&nbsp;</td><td>&nbsp;</td></tr><tr><td id="etext4" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;font-family:Arial;background-color:#f4f4f4">British moves against Russian interests in the form of asset freezes, bars from City of London exchanges and a full embargo on all current or prospective arms contracts could help meet French concerns about unequal burden-sharing.</td><td id="ctext4" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;background-color:#f4f4f4">英国如果针对俄罗斯利益集团采取冻结资产、禁止进入伦敦金融城(City of London)交易所、对现有或未来武器合约全面禁运的措施，可能有助于解决法国对于负担不平等的担忧。</td></tr><tr><td>&nbsp;</td><td>&nbsp;</td></tr><tr><td id="etext5" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;font-family:Arial;background-color:#f4f4f4">Second, the unilateral cancellation of the Mistral contract could be used by competitors to tar the French as unreliable suppliers, for instance in long-running talks over the multibillion-dollar sale of Rafale combat aircraft to India. A solution would be for partners from Nato or the EU participate in the cancellation, by agreeing to purchase the two ships for a symbolic price of a euro per country when legal circumstances permit. That would make it hard for other countries to argue that they are more reliable.</td><td id="ctext5" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;background-color:#f4f4f4">其次，单边取消“西北风”级战舰合约可能会导致竞争对手将法国抹黑为不可靠的供应者，例如在向印度出售数十亿美元阵风(Rafale)战斗机的长期谈判中。一种解决方案是，北约或欧盟成员国参与到交易取消中来，同意在法律条件允许时，以每个国家1欧元的象征性价格购买这两艘战舰。这将令其他国家很难声称他们更可靠。</td></tr><tr><td>&nbsp;</td><td>&nbsp;</td></tr><tr><td id="etext6" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;font-family:Arial;background-color:#f4f4f4">Last, it is not clear that the French authorities fully understand how seriously their central European partners would react to the delivery of the Mistrals. Poland is spending ￠20bn in the next few years to modernise its military. French industry is competing for, and is in some instances already shortlisted for, air defence, combat helicopter and submarine contracts. If the Mistrals were delivered, not only would these prospects evaporate but the rapidly developing French-Polish relationship would suffer immense moral and political harm.</td><td id="ctext6" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;background-color:#f4f4f4">最后，目前还不清楚，法国政府是否完全了解其中欧合作伙伴将对“西北风”级战舰交付做出何等严肃的反应。未来几年，波兰将支出200亿欧元实行军队现代化。法国工业正在争夺空中防御、战斗直升机以及潜水艇合约，有的法国企业已进入这些合约的决选名单。如果“西北风”级战舰交付，不仅这些前景将成为泡影，法国与波兰迅速发展的关系也将遭受巨大的道德和政治损害。</td></tr><tr><td>&nbsp;</td><td>&nbsp;</td></tr><tr><td id="etext7" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;font-family:Arial;background-color:#f4f4f4">It is imperative that this deal does not go forward, and it is in the general interest of the western allies to help France overcome the obstacles to the cancellation of this misbegotten contract.</td><td id="ctext7" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;background-color:#f4f4f4">务必不能让这笔交易继续推进，帮助法国克服取消这一可鄙合约的障碍，符合西方盟友的总体利益。</td></tr><tr><td>&nbsp;</td><td>&nbsp;</td></tr><tr><td id="etext8" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;font-family:Arial;background-color:#f4f4f4">There is a broader lesson from this mess. Although sanctions against Russia are symbolically and practically useful, they have two basic drawbacks. They are self-punitive in that they hurt the sanctioner, in terms of lost jobs, money or energy supplies, and not simply the target. They are deeply divisive, as is made abundantly clear by the political links made between proposed Mistral cancellation and the failure to punish Londongrad.</td><td id="ctext8" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;background-color:#f4f4f4">这个烂摊子给人们带来了更广泛的教训。尽管从象征意义和实际意义上来说，针对俄罗斯的制裁是有用的，但这些制裁存在两个基本缺陷。第一，制裁具有自我惩罚性，它们不仅会损害被制裁国，制裁国的就业、资金或能源供应也会受损。第二，制裁存在严重分歧，在拟议的取消“西北风”级战舰交易和“伦敦格勒”未能受到惩罚之间存在政治关联，就非常明确地体现出了这点。</td></tr><tr><td>&nbsp;</td><td>&nbsp;</td></tr><tr><td id="etext9" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;font-family:Arial;background-color:#f4f4f4">The west has more to gain from focusing its efforts on supporting Ukraine economically, politically and militarily, as we did with neutral countries such as Sweden or Finland in the cold war. This is the most direct way of preventing the establishment of a new Russian empire, which should be our objective. Punishing Russia is not a strategic goal in its own right. Sanctions are important; support for Ukraine is essential.</td><td id="ctext9" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;background-color:#f4f4f4">集中精力在经济、政治和军事上支持乌克兰，就像我们在冷战期间对待瑞典或芬兰等中立国家那样，将让西方获益更多。这是阻止一个新的俄罗斯帝国建立起来的最直接方式，这应该是我们的目标。惩罚俄罗斯本身并非一项战略目标。制裁很重要；支持乌克兰更是至关重要。</td></tr><tr><td>&nbsp;</td><td>&nbsp;</td></tr><tr><td id="etext10" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;font-family:Arial;background-color:#f4f4f4">The writer is a special adviser at the Fondation pour la Recherche Stratégique, a think-tank in Paris</td><td id="ctext10" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;background-color:#f4f4f4"><i>注：本文作者是巴黎智库法国战略研究基金会(Fondation pour la Recherche Stratégique)特别顾问。</i></td></tr><tr><td>&nbsp;</td><td>&nbsp;</td></tr><tr><td id="etext11" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;font-family:Arial;background-color:#f4f4f4"><!-- 记录内容加载时间 --><ftclear><strong></strong></ftclear></td><td id="ctext11" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;background-color:#f4f4f4">译者/梁亚楠</td></tr><tr><td>&nbsp;</td><td>&nbsp;</td></tr><tr><td id="etext12" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;font-family:Arial;background-color:#f4f4f4">&nbsp;</td><td id="ctext12" align="left" valign="top" width="49%" style="font-size:16px;background-color:#f4f4f4"><!-- 记录内容加载时间 --></td></tr></tbody></table></div>
